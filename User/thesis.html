Optimizing Machine Learning Algorithms for Real-Time Data Processing in IoT Network

Date:

December 16 , 2024

Author:


Alex Morgan, PhD



Alex Morgan, PhD



Alex Morgan, PhD


Where:



2nd floor liceria library, section E



Abstract:


The rapid expansion of the Internet of Things (IoT) has led to an unprecedented influx of real-time data, necessitating the development of optimized machine learning (ML) algorithms capable of processing this data efficiently. This study explores the integration of ML techniques with IoT networks, focusing on the challenges and solutions associated with real-time data processing. By analyzing the performance of various ML algorithms, the research identifies key optimization strategies to enhance speed, accuracy, and resource management in IoT environments. The proposed framework aims to improve decision-making processes in IoT networks, enabling more responsive and intelligent systems. Through simulations and real-world applications, the study demonstrates the effectiveness of these optimized algorithms in maintaining the balance between computational efficiency and predictive accuracy, ultimately contributing to the advancement of smart, data-driven IoT systems.

Objectives:

To optimize machine learning algorithms for real-time data processing in IoT networks by identifying challenges, developing and validating enhancements, and assessing their impact on system efficiency and intelligence.

Methods:

The research will employ a combination of algorithmic analysis, simulation testing, and real-world case studies to develop and evaluate optimization techniques for improving the performance of machine learning algorithms in real-time IoT data processing.

Conclusion:

Optimized ML algorithms significantly enhance real-time IoT network performance.
